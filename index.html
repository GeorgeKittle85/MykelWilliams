Writing
Tips

6 rules that separate good writing from great writing.

01
Kill the passive voice
"The ball was caught by Kittle" is weaker than "Kittle caught the ball." Active voice hits harder, every time.
02
One idea per sentence
Don't cram two routes into one play. Short, focused sentences build momentum and aid comprehension.
03
Vary sentence length
Short punches. Then a longer, flowing sentence that gives the reader room to absorb the idea before moving on.
04
Cut filler words
"Very," "really," "basically," and "just" are like holding penalties — they slow the play down without adding anything.
05
Strong verbs win
Kittle doesn't "go" across the field — he sprints, cuts, or bulldozes. Precise verbs paint the picture instantly.
06
Read it aloud
If you stumble reading it, your reader will too. Your ear catches awkward phrasing that your eyes skip right over.
